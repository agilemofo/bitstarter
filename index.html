Ever wondered why Pappy Van Winkle is so special? Trying to figure out what to do when your mother-in-law asks to make her a martini with that nice bottle of gin she gave you as a Christmas present last year? Sick of Bud Light but have no idea what 'Hops' is?

Maestro is a simple tool for helping you catalog and discover new drinks and explore awesome new up-and-coming bars and restaurants. Whether you enjoy drinking cocktails, wine, or beer, Maestro demystifies your favorite beverages and teaches how to recreate them at home.

Why Maestro?

  • Maestro Concierge

    Tell Maestro a bit about what you're in the mood for and he will suggest the ideal accompaniment

  • Little Black Book

    Make a note or snap a quick photo of a particular vintage for future reference. Out with the boss when he orders his favorite pinot? Order an absurdly nice Champagne to toast your engagement? Drinks are often the perfect pairing for some of life's most cherished moments. Impress people by serving up a taste of that experience

  • The Library

    Farm to Table is a concept not just for foodies. Your favorite beverages are grown by an increasing array of successful small farms and are produced by an army of independent brewers, distillers, vintners and baristas. Learn the difference between bourbon and rye and why double fermentation may change the way you feel about sparkling wine. Also peruse tips, tricks and recipes from some of the most influential bartenders the world has ever known

  • My Collection

    Track your current supplies and favorite recipes. Selectively share and swap brews and bottles with friends and family.

FAQ

Who is Maestro for?
Maestro is for beverage lovers of all kinds. There has been a lot of attention paid to where your food comes from and the stories and livelihoods of the farmers and producers that help bring it to you. Maestro is trying to raise a similar level of awareness to the efforts and livelihood of the small farmers and producers that work to bring you fresh coffee, small batch bourbons, and craft beer. Maestro's goal is to help you learn more about these great ingredients and teach you how to use them in simple and exciting ways.
When will Maestro be available?
Maestro is currently beta testing with a small number of users. We hope to open for business in the Fall of 2013!
